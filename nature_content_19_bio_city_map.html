Biophilic cities are the idea of incorporating nature into the urban world. Biomimicry, or nature as inspiration, incorporates the genius of the natural world into our own problem solving. Biodesign, or nature as solution, incorporates live organisms or plant life into our problem solving process. This website illustrates the possibilites in which we could see biomimicry and biodesign in our everyday urban environments at several scales: the daily objects that we interact with, the buildings that reside in, and the flow of the cities themselves.

BIO CITY MAP

  • Terreform ONE
  • New York
  • 2013

The BioCity Map predicts world population density through the use of bacteria.

The use of E. coli in a petri dish displays population density onto a large map. The three dimensional map has predicted that within the next 100 years, the world population will reach 11 billion people. The project involved the collaborative work of urban developers and planners, cartogrophers, biologists and architects, and it focused on the current 25 cities with the largest population densities.
